A breezy install.

Just two steps with LESS.

Be sure to use the LESS version of Boostrap.

  1. Include scheme.less anywhere.

  2. Include 1pxdeep.less after all Bootstrap LESS.

A working index.less might look like this:

@import "scheme.less"; // color scheme
@import "bootstrap.less"; // bootstrap
@import "responsive.less"; // bootstrap responsive
@import "1pxdeep.less"; // 1pxdeep theme
@import "style.less"; // your own styles

Need help with LESS? Try the LESS CSS project page.

It's all relative.

Get to work with schemes; don't lollygag with colors.

Don't design for specific colors; design for schemes. Your colors might change tomorrow, anyway.

Need to rebrand? Save your current layout with the change of just one LESS variable.

Just plain indecisive? Great. Decide later.

SchemeLESS.

Work as much or as little on scheming as you want to.

Edit scheme.less to edit your color scheme.

Not touching anything gives you a @seed-color of #ed8c2b, which generates this page's default scheme.

Edit @seed-color to get an entirely new scheme. Tweak from there, editing the color wheel, luma breaks and mix-ins until you're happy with the results.

You can even overwrite colors entirely— a great way to passive-aggressively commentate on the quality of SchemeLESS's default scheme generator.

Some helpful tips.

It's still on you to make something that looks great.

  • 1pxdeep generally works better with a @seed-color that's on the less saturated side (the left side of the picker below).

  • Using lots of wells will give depth and structure to the page, while giving you more control over background colors.

  • Any text/readability issues can usually be sorted quickly with the .contrast(@color) mix-in, which accepts its intended background color as an argument.

  • Most elements are white without a color class, a look which may or may not work for your site.

  • Compile your LESS! This page is only running client-side LESS because the author likes to live dangerously.
